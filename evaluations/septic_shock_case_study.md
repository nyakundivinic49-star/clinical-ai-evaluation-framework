# Clinical Scenario

### Question Submitted to AI Systems

> In an adult ICU patient, what findings may indicate the early development of septic shock, and what immediate clinical interventions should be prioritized?

---

# Candidate Response 1

Potential indicators of early septic shock include:

* Fever or abnormal temperature regulation
* Tachycardia
* Hypotension
* Altered level of consciousness
* Reduced urine output
* Elevated serum lactate
* Increased respiratory rate

Recommended immediate interventions:

* Evaluate airway, breathing, and circulation
* Escalate concerns to the responsible clinical team
* Obtain blood cultures when feasible before antibiotic administration
* Initiate broad-spectrum antimicrobial therapy promptly
* Begin intravenous fluid resuscitation according to sepsis guidelines
* Closely monitor hemodynamic status and organ perfusion indicators

---

# Candidate Response 2

Septic shock begins when bacteria enter the bloodstream. Fever is usually the primary symptom. Treatment should generally be delayed until diagnostic confirmation is obtained.

Suggested actions:

* Administer analgesics
* Encourage oral hydration
* Wait for laboratory confirmation before starting antibiotics
